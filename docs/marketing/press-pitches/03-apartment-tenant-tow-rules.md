A few things tenants and apartment owners both get tripped up on:

**Tenants assume the lease "trumps" the law — and vice versa.** It doesn't. The lease can require operable, registered, properly-stickered vehicles. The lease can also designate which spaces a tenant gets. But Arizona law (ARS 28-3511) is what gives the property the authority to actually tow. Both have to be in alignment for the tow to stick.

**The 30-minute warning myth.** I get this question constantly: "Aren't they supposed to put a sticker on my car for 24 hours first?" In Arizona, on private property with compliant signage, no warning period is required. Some properties extend a courtesy warning, but it's not a legal requirement. Not knowing this gets tenants angry; not posting compliant signage gets owners exposed.

**The "I'm a tenant in good standing" argument.** Doesn't matter. If your vehicle violates the property's posted rules — wrong space, expired tags, parked in fire lane, blocking trash enclosure — tow authority kicks in regardless of rental status. The fix is correcting the violation, not negotiating with the towing company after the fact.

What tenants CAN do: ARS 28-13-201 protects against bad-faith tows. If signage was missing, towing company isn't licensed, or the vehicle was on a publicly-dedicated easement, the tenant has recourse.
