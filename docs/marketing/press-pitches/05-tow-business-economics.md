The Arizona PPI (private property impound) model:

**The property pays nothing.** Apartment community, HOA, commercial building — none of them pay us. Counterintuitively, they're our customers without being our payers. That's deliberately built into the statute.

**The vehicle owner pays.** Standard tow + first-day storage + mileage runs $200-400 in Phoenix metro. Storage accumulates daily until the vehicle is reclaimed or sold at auction. Average claim time in our yard: 4-7 days. Median bill at claim: $325.

**Auction is the lever.** Vehicles unclaimed at 30 days go to ADOT lien filing. After lien closes (~60-90 days from tow), the towing company takes ownership and sells via auction. Most vehicles sold this way recover less than their accumulated fees — meaning the towing company eats the loss. The economics only work when claim rate stays above ~70%.

**Profit margin is thin.** Industry average is 3-7% net margin on revenue. Trucks cost $90-150K each and depreciate fast. Driver wages, fuel, insurance, yard rent, ADOT lien filings, auction fees — eats most revenue. The "tow companies are getting rich" framing doesn't match the books.

**Where the predatory accusation comes from** — companies that rely on "spotter" tows (driving lots looking for violations vs. responding to property calls) and overstated mileage. Both are illegal under ARS 9-499.05 in Arizona but not always enforced. That's the actual abuse vector — not the model itself.
