Three things HOA boards routinely get wrong about towing:

**1. They wait too long.** Most CC&Rs grant immediate authority to remove unauthorized vehicles, but boards typically negotiate, send warnings, post notes — for weeks. Meanwhile every other resident sees the violator getting away with it, and enforcement credibility erodes. Once you have signage, the law (ARS 28-3511) lets you act on day one.

**2. They confuse "abandoned" with "unauthorized."** These are two different statutes with different processes. An unauthorized vehicle (parked in a fire lane, blocking dumpster, in a numbered space without sticker) can be removed immediately. An abandoned vehicle (inoperable, expired tags 90+ days, no claimed owner) requires a longer ARS 28-4141 process. Towing companies that don't explain this leave boards exposed to liability.

**3. They think they're paying for it.** Under Arizona's PPI model, the property never pays for tows on its own lot. Fees are recovered from the vehicle owner. The HOA's only cost is signage installation (one-time) and member time spent enforcing. Boards budgeting for "towing costs" usually have a vendor charging them — that's not the standard model in Arizona.
