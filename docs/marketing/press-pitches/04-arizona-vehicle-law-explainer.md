Quick map of the four statutes that govern most vehicle/parking issues on private property in Arizona:

**ARS 28-3511** — Removal of unauthorized vehicles from private property. Property owners with compliant signage can authorize immediate removal. This is the everyday workhorse statute.

**ARS 28-4141 through 28-4145** — Abandoned vehicle definition, removal procedures, and ADOT-managed lien process. Used when there's no signage authority OR the vehicle is genuinely abandoned (not just parked wrong).

**ARS 9-499.05** — Municipal towing regulations. Adds signage specs, fee caps, licensing requirements, and notification rules within incorporated city limits. Applies on top of (not instead of) ARS 28-3511.

**ARS 28-872** — Parking violations on public roads. NOT the same as private property towing — public-road tows are governed by city/state, not the property owner. Common reporter mix-up.

What I see often: stories conflating ARS 28-3511 with ARS 28-4141 (immediate authority vs abandoned-vehicle process), or assuming Arizona requires a 24-72hr warning period (it doesn't, on properly signed private property).
