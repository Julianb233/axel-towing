The single biggest misconception I hear from property owners is that they're "stuck" with an abandoned vehicle until the registered owner comes back. That's wrong in Arizona. Two statutes give them immediate authority:

**ARS 28-3511** — The fast lane. If a property has compliant signage at every vehicle entrance (towing company name, phone, impound yard address), an unauthorized vehicle can be towed *immediately* with no waiting period. Most property owners don't know this exists.

**ARS 28-4141 through 28-4145** — The abandoned-vehicle lane. Used when there's no signage or the vehicle clearly meets the abandonment definition (inoperable, expired registration 90+ days, signs of intentional desertion). Slower process but ends in the towing company filing a mechanic's lien through ADOT.

The catch most people miss: in Arizona, all of this is **free to the property owner.** Fees are recovered from the vehicle owner under the PPI (private property impound) model. So the typical "I can't afford to deal with it" hesitation isn't actually true — it's a procedure issue, not a money issue.
