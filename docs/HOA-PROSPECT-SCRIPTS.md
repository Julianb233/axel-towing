## How to Use This Document

These scripts are ready to use. Replace all `[bracketed]` fields with real information before sending. Never send a template without personalizing it — prospects can identify a mass message immediately.

**General rules:**

- Stop the sequence the moment someone replies — switch to a real conversation
- Never send all three LinkedIn touches in the same week — space them out as indicated
- Always track sends, opens, and replies in your CRM (GHL)
- If someone says "not interested," respond politely and remove from sequence — do not follow up again
- Phone calls should only go to warm leads (people who have replied or shown interest)

## Part 3: Phone Call Scripts for Warm Leads

### When to call: Use phone ONLY for warm leads (people who have replied to LinkedIn or email, or who have expressed interest via any channel). Cold-calling is low-ROI for this segment.

**Before every call:**

- Review their LinkedIn profile and any notes from your CRM
- Know which community or company they are associated with
- Have your calendar open for scheduling
- Have the axletowing.com URL ready to text/email them a relevant resource after the call

---

### Call Script C-A: HOA Board Member (Warm Lead)

**Scenario:** They replied to a LinkedIn DM or email expressing interest

**Opening:**

```
"Hi [First Name], this is Elliott with Axle Towing & Impound. You
replied to my message about parking enforcement for [Community Name] —
thanks for getting back to me. Is now a good time for a quick conversation?
```

_(If yes, continue. If no, ask: "When's a better time? I can call back whenever works for you.")_

**Discovery questions (ask 2-3, then listen):**

```
"Great. I just have a few questions so I can understand what [Community Name]
is dealing with — is that okay?

→ "What does your current parking enforcement situation look like?
   Are you working with a towing company now, or is it kind of ad hoc?"

→ "What are the biggest parking complaints you're hearing from residents
   right now?"

→ "Has the board tried to address parking enforcement before? What happened?"

→ "Is this something the board is actively looking to solve, or more of
   a 'we'd like to eventually' situation?"
```

_(Listen carefully. The answers tell you exactly how to frame your pitch.)_

**Value statement (tailor based on what they said):**

```
"Here's how we typically work with communities like yours:

We do a free property walkthrough — takes about 20 minutes. I walk
every entrance, check the signage, identify the high-violation zones,
and put together a simple enforcement recommendation.

If the board decides to move forward, we install compliant towing
signs, set up a patrol schedule, and start enforcement — all at zero
cost to the HOA. Our fee comes from tow impound fees, not from the
association budget.

The board doesn't have to approve a budget line, and residents stop
getting away with violations. Would that kind of assessment be useful?"
```

**Handling common objections:**

_"We already have a towing company."_

```
"That's totally fair — most communities do. I'm not here to displace
anyone just for the sake of it. Would you be open to having us do a
free signage compliance check alongside your current provider? If your
signs are fully compliant, great — I'll tell you so and we'll part
ways. If there are gaps, you'll want to know regardless of who you
use for towing."
```

_"We're on a fixed HOA budget and can't add expenses."_

```
"I completely understand HOA budget constraints. The good news is
our service has zero cost to the HOA. We're paid through tow impound
fees collected from violators — the people who park illegally cover
our service. There's no line item on the HOA budget."
```

_"We've had bad experiences with towing companies."_

```
"I hear that more than you might think. Most of the time it comes
down to lack of accountability — the towing company shows up when
they feel like it, doesn't provide reports, and the board has no idea
what's happening.

We work differently: you get monthly enforcement reports, my direct
cell number, and 30-minute response times. If something ever goes
wrong, you call me directly — not a call center.

Would you be willing to try a 30-day trial? If after 30 days you're
not satisfied, we remove our signs and you're no worse off than before."
```

**Closing for the assessment:**

```
"Let me suggest this: let me come out and do the free property
walkthrough. I'll give you an honest assessment of what I see —
whether it's a simple signage fix or a full enforcement program.

You can share that with the board and make a decision with real
information in front of you. Does [day] or [day] work this week?
I can usually do evenings or weekends if that's easier for you as
a volunteer board member."
```

**Call close:**

```
"Perfect. I'll send you a confirmation and my direct cell. If anything
comes up before then, just text or call me at 480-288-5526. And I'll
send over a quick resource on Arizona towing law requirements for
HOA boards — useful to have before we meet.

Thanks for your time, [First Name]. See you [day/time]."
```

### Call Script C-C: Post-Assessment Follow-Up Call

**Scenario:** You completed a free property assessment and are following up to move toward a contract

**Opening:**

```
"Hi [First Name], this is Elliott with Axle Towing. I wanted to
follow up on the property walkthrough we did at [Community Name]
last [day]. Do you have a few minutes to go over what I found?"
```

**Summary of findings (adjust to what you actually found):**

```
"So here's what I noted during the assessment:

[Walk through 2-4 specific findings. Be specific and factual.]

For example:
- 'You have 3 entrances to the community, and one of them on the
  east side doesn't have a compliant towing sign. That's a liability
  gap.'
- 'The visitor parking area near building C had 6 unauthorized vehicles
  when I walked it at 10am on a Tuesday — that's consistent with
  what you're seeing.'
- 'Your current sign says the enforcement company name but doesn't
  list a phone number, which is required under ARS 9-499.05.'

None of these are deal-breakers, but they're worth addressing if
you want enforcement to hold up legally."
```

**Moving toward the contract:**

```
"Here's what I'd propose: We install compliant signage at all
[X] entrances — we provide and install everything, no cost to
the HOA. Then we start on a patrol schedule you and the board
agree to.

There's no contract required to start — we can do a 30-day trial
so you can show the board results before committing to anything
long-term.

Does that work for your situation, or would the board prefer to
see a formal proposal first?"
```

**If they need board approval:**

```
"Completely understand — most HOA decisions go through the board.
Here's what I can do: I'll put together a one-page summary of
what I found in the assessment and the recommended enforcement
plan. You can present it at the next board meeting.

If the board has any questions, I'm happy to join a call or even
attend the meeting if that would help. Board members always have
questions, and having me there to answer them directly moves
things faster.

When is your next board meeting? Let's time the proposal so it's
in front of them as soon as possible."
```
