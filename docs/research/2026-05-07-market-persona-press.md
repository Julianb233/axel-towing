### 1B. Three Property Manager Buyer Personas

#### Persona A — "The Big-Portfolio Operator"
Example archetypes: Rachael Kish (Asset Living), Stacey Hogue (Greystar), Adam Greco (Mark-Taylor), Amanda Shaw (AAM), Tim Garvin (City Property).

- **What they care about:** Risk reduction, brand consistency across 50-700 properties, no surprises in the news. They will sacrifice 10-15% on price for a vendor who will not embarrass them.
- **What triggers a switch:** A single bad incident — a wrongful tow making the local news, a tow truck damaging a resident's car, a TikTok video going viral, a slow response causing a tenant safety issue. One bad event, and they're shopping immediately.
- **Budget process:** Vendor selection is annual or biennial; usually a corporate procurement RFP-style process for the top 3 firms. Smaller firms have a regional director who decides without an RFP.
- **What they fear:** Compliance violations under HB2305 (state law now). HOA-board lawsuits (ARS § 9-499.05(B) creates a presumption AGAINST private-property towing unless the property owner has authorized it correctly). Negative reviews tying their brand to "predatory towing."
- **How they evaluate:** Insurance certificate (COI), licensing (AZ DPS tow truck registration + AZ ROC if applicable), response time SLA, accessible online portal for tow records, direct hotline to a manager (NOT a 1-800 dispatch). Yard cleanliness on a site visit is a non-negotiable signal.

#### Persona B — "The Independent Apartment Owner / Boutique Operator"
Example archetypes: Reid Butler (Butler Housing), Crystal DeHoag (Bella Group), Ann Diaz (AZ 1st Realty), Jaren Bradley (JBA Management), Kim Pacheco (Scotia Group).

- **What they care about:** Same-day responsiveness, a real human who picks up the phone, no surprise fees, willingness to work with their specific signage and policy quirks.
- **What triggers a switch:** Their incumbent vendor missed a call; a tenant complained about a tow that was technically legal but was bad PR; a competitor offered them a better contract or a free sign installation.
- **Budget process:** No RFP. The owner-operator decides on a phone call. Decision in <30 days from first contact.
- **What they fear:** Liability. HOA/tenant disputes that escalate into a lawsuit. Bad Google reviews tagged to their property.
- **How they evaluate:** Single phone call. Are they professional? Do they understand my building? Will they show up at 2am on a Saturday? Did they propose anything I hadn't thought of (e.g. parking pass system, signage upgrade, free site walk)?

#### Persona C — "The HOA Board Treasurer"
Example archetypes: A volunteer board member of a 200-unit condo association — typically a retired professional, an attorney, or a real-estate broker — who serves on the HOA board.

- **What they care about:** Resident complaints, predictability of expenses, NOT getting personally sued. Towing is a 2nd-order concern delegated to the HOA management company.
- **What triggers a switch:** Resident lawsuits or complaints; a state-law change (like HB2305) making the board worried about compliance; a board election where the new board wants to "clean up the parking situation."
- **Budget process:** The HOA board approves the management company's vendor recommendation. Budget approval is annual at a board meeting. Towing isn't a line item — it's invisible (it's enforced via signage; the towing company gets paid by the vehicle owner, not the HOA).
- **What they fear:** A wrongful-tow lawsuit ending up at the board's door. Bad press in the local paper. The towing company holding the HOA hostage on rates.
- **How they evaluate:** Their HOA management company's recommendation. Reviews. Whether the towing company has caused a single problem in 12 months. **Implication: pitch HOA managers, not HOA boards directly.**

## Part 2 — Industry Trends Brief 2024-2026

### Trend 1 — HB2305 Centralized Arizona Towing Regulation (2024-25, biggest shift in 15 years)

In 2024, Arizona passed HB2305, removing the ability of counties, cities, and towns to set their own maximum rates and charges for private-property towing. There is now a **single statewide framework** governing towing carrier rates, signage, post-tow notification windows, vehicle release procedures, and overcharge penalties. Penalties include reimbursement plus 2x the towing and storage fees for violations. Successor bill HB2305 (2026 session) and SB1059 / SB1127 (2025) further refined the framework. (Source: azleg.gov, billtrack50.com, legiscan.com.)

**What this means for Axle:** every Phoenix-metro property manager is now operating under a brand-new compliance regime. There is a 12-24 month window where "the operator who knows the new law" is a meaningful differentiator. Marketing should anchor on this. Most competitors (including Freeway Towing) are still quoting old city ordinances on their websites.

### Trend 2 — ARS § 9-499.05(B) Establishes Presumption Against Private-Property Towing

Per Arizona Revised Statutes 9-499.05(B), towing or immobilization on private property is **not automatically permitted**. The statute creates a presumption against it unless the property owner or authorized agent has explicitly approved it with required signage and authorization. CHDB Law's 2024 analysis flags this as a major HOA-board liability area. This is a perfect "expert column" angle for Axle's owner Elliott — "What HOA boards need to know before authorizing a tow."

### Trend 3 — Phoenix Multifamily Hit Record Net Absorption in 2024-25

Per Cushman & Wakefield Q3 2025: Phoenix multifamily net absorption hit 12,128 units in the first 3 quarters of 2025, on track for the strongest year on record. 24,000 new units delivered in the past 12 months, 23,000 more under construction. (Source: getmultifamily.com, cushmanwakefield.com, MMG Real Estate Advisors.)

**What this means:** Phoenix has more apartment communities being built and lease-up'd than at any point in the metro's history. Each new community needs a private-property tow vendor on day one of operations. That's a constant pipeline of new prospects — but it also means Greystar, Mark-Taylor, and Avenue5 are signing fresh master towing agreements right now.

### Trend 4 — Vacancy Rate Up, Rent Growth Negative — Property Owners Are Cost-Sensitive

Q3 2025 Phoenix vacancy: 12.4%. Rent growth: -2.8%. (Source: Matthews Q3 2025.) Rising vacancy + falling rents means property managers are under margin pressure. The implication: they will not sign a 3-year exclusive master towing contract without aggressive terms (or strong differentiation). Axle should expect price pushback and counter with service-level differentiation, not price.

### Trend 5 — Parking Management Apps Are NOT Displacing Private-Property Towing Yet

ParkMobile, ParkM (multifamily-focused), Reef Technology, FlashParking, and SpotHero are growing in Phoenix, but the market is bifurcating: parking apps handle paid/permit parking management; towing handles enforcement of unauthorized parking. The two are complementary, not competitive. ParkM specifically markets to multifamily and integrates with towing vendors. **Strategic angle:** Axle should publicly position as the "enforcement partner" to parking apps — appearing in their integration docs would be a citation/SEO win.

### Trend 6 — APTRA & Tow Operators Safety Coalition Are Active in Arizona

The Arizona Professional Towing and Recovery Association (aptra.net) and the Tow Operators Safety Coalition (towopsafe.org) are the two state-level industry bodies. APTRA hosted a 2024 webinar on HB2305 with attorney Conor Gleason. **Implication:** Elliott should be a member of APTRA if he isn't already, and contributed content / panel speakers from APTRA's webinar are direct PR opportunities.

### Trend 7 — Insurance/Liability Pressure on Tow Operators is Rising

Federal-level conversation on tow truck operator safety is escalating after a series of struck-by-vehicle deaths nationally; Arizona is among the states tightening Move Over enforcement. Property managers reading the news are more cost-aware on insurance certificates than ever. **Implication:** Axle's COI, $1M-$2M liability coverage, AZ DPS tow truck registration, and APTRA membership should be displayed prominently on the website and in every cold email — that's a 30-second trust signal that closes deals.

### Trend 8 — Apache Junction Towing Contracts Were Re-Bid in 2024

The Apache Junction City Council voted on Feb 20, 2024 to extend towing contracts through June 30 (extending the previous Apache Sands etc. contracts), and Phoenix Metro Towing requested expansion. **What it means for Axle's Apache Junction yard (1151 W Apache Trail, 85120):** there's an active conversation at city level about who tows for the city and how — Axle should be on Apache Junction's vendor list and at their council meetings.
