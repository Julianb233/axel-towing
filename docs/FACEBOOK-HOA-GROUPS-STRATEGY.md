## Why Facebook HOA Groups

HOA board members are dramatically more active in Facebook community groups than on LinkedIn. These groups are where they:

- Ask for vendor recommendations ("does anyone have a good towing company?")
- Vent about parking problems
- Discuss compliance and legal questions
- Network with board members at nearby communities

A well-executed Facebook group strategy puts Axle Towing in front of decision makers who are actively experiencing the exact pain Axle solves — at the moment they're looking for a solution.

## Part 3: Phase-by-Phase Execution

### Phase 1: Join and Observe (Weeks 1–4)

**Goal:** Understand the community, identify recurring pain points, find the most active members.

**Actions:**

1. Request to join 8–12 groups using the list above
2. Complete any group admin questions honestly (e.g., "Are you a homeowner? Yes / No") — note: even if you're a service provider, many groups accept business owners. Be honest about who you are.
3. Once approved, do NOT post or comment for the first 5–7 days
4. Read through recent posts (past 30–60 days) — take notes on:
   - How often parking violations come up
   - What language board members use ("unauthorized vehicle," "towing company," "CC&R enforcement")
   - What complaints people have about existing towing vendors
   - Who the most active and trusted voices are in each group

**What to track in a simple spreadsheet:**

- Group name
- Member count
- Posting frequency (daily / weekly / occasional)
- Quality of parking-related discussions (high / medium / low)
- Key active members worth following

### Phase 2: Add Value Without Selling (Weeks 5–8)

**Goal:** Become a trusted contributor. Build name recognition before any mention of Axle Towing.

**Types of posts and comments to engage with:**

**1. Parking enforcement questions**
When someone asks: "Does anyone know if our HOA can legally tow from visitor parking?"
Post a comment like:

> "Yes — Arizona HOAs have explicit towing authority under ARS § 33-1818 as long as the CC&Rs permit it and compliant signage is in place. Most disputes happen because of improper signage, not the tow itself. Happy to share the specific signage requirements if that's useful."

**2. Towing complaint posts**
When someone says: "Our towing company is impossible to reach — anyone have recommendations?"
Do NOT immediately pitch Axle. Instead:

> "That's unfortunately common. What city are you in? The coverage area matters a lot for response time. Also worth asking any vendor for their average response time in writing before signing — it varies a lot."

(This positions you as helpful without being promotional. You can follow up with a DM if they engage.)

**3. Legal / compliance questions**
When someone asks: "Do we need signs before we can tow?"
Post a helpful, specific answer:

> "Yes — ARS § 28-4843 has specific requirements for private property towing signage. Signs must be clearly visible at all entrances, specify the towing company and phone number, include authorization language, and meet size/font requirements. If you want, I can share the exact requirements — a lot of communities don't know they're not in compliance until there's a dispute."

**4. General community questions**
Comment on non-towing topics occasionally (local events, neighborhood safety questions) to appear as a well-rounded community member, not just a towing resource.

---

**Original posts to share in groups (Week 5–8):**

Only post original content after you've been commenting for 2+ weeks and have some community presence.

Post 1: Educational resource

> "I've been helping a few HOA boards around [City] review their parking enforcement setup and noticed a lot of communities are unknowingly out of compliance with Arizona signage requirements. Happy to share a quick checklist if anyone wants it — just comment below or DM me. No strings."

Post 2: Question to the group

> "Curious for HOA board members here — what's your biggest pain point with parking enforcement right now? Resident complaints? Documentation? Finding a reliable towing vendor? Trying to understand what the most common issues are."

Post 3: Helpful tip (non-promotional)

> "Quick tip for HOA board members: if you ever have a tow disputed, the most important thing you can have is a photo of the vehicle in the violation zone WITH your parking sign visible in the same frame. That single piece of documentation resolves 90% of disputes. Just something worth building into whatever enforcement process you have."

### Phase 3: Soft Introduction (Weeks 9–12)

**Goal:** Introduce Axle Towing organically, respond to direct recommendation requests, convert group members to leads.

**Responding to direct recommendation requests:**
When someone posts: "Looking for a reliable towing company for our HOA in Chandler — anyone have recommendations?"

Respond (briefly, first-person):

Key principles for this response:

- Be brief — don't write an advertisement in the comments
- Don't lead with a pitch — offer to connect, not a sales brochure
- Let others recommend you if they know you (the ideal scenario)

**Gentle mention in educational posts:**
After you've built credibility in the group:

> "I've worked with a few boards in [Area] on this exact issue — parking enforcement compliance, documentation, and keeping residents from disputing every tow. Happy to share what we've seen work if anyone wants to connect."

## Part 4: Group Moderation Rules to Follow

Facebook HOA groups have moderators who will remove you if you're too promotional. Follow these rules strictly.

1. **No direct advertising in post text.** Do not write posts that read like ads. If you mention Axle Towing, it should be in the context of a helpful answer, not a pitch.

2. **No repeated posting.** Post original content in a group no more than 2–3 times per month. Over-posting = spam flag.

3. **No posting the same content across multiple groups.** Moderators in interconnected HOA circles talk to each other.

4. **No "check our website" or "call us today" language.** This triggers spam filters and moderator attention.

5. **Respond to every comment on your posts.** Active engagement signals to admins that you're a genuine community participant.

6. **Never debate or argue in groups.** If someone pushes back on something you said, respond once with facts and move on. Don't escalate.

7. **Read each group's rules before posting.** Some groups explicitly prohibit vendor promotion. If so, do not post promotional content — comments only, and only genuinely helpful ones.

8. **Don't DM members without some prior group interaction.** Cold DMs from Facebook group members feel invasive. Comment first, DM only after they've engaged with you.

## Part 5: Converting Group Members to Leads

The path from Facebook group member to Axle Towing client typically looks like this:

**Stage 1:** They see your helpful comment on someone else's post
**Stage 2:** They click your profile — your professional profile builds trust
**Stage 3:** They comment on one of your posts or DM you with a question
**Stage 4:** You respond helpfully, offer a free resource (signage checklist, ARS overview)
**Stage 5:** You offer a free signage audit or 15-minute call
**Stage 6:** Call booked → proposal → contract

**DM template for Facebook group leads:**

> Hi [First Name] — thanks for reaching out. Happy to help.
>
> [Answer their question or acknowledge their situation briefly.]
>
> If you want, I can do a quick free audit of your current parking enforcement setup — just a 15-minute call. I'll tell you what's compliant, what's not, and what it would take to tighten things up. No sales pitch involved — just useful information.
>
> Would that be helpful?

Separate from the group strategy, the Axle Towing business page should be active and professional.

**Posting cadence:** 3–4x per week (align with Instagram — repurpose content)
**Content types:**
