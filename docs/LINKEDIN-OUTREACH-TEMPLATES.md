## How to Use These Templates

1. **Never copy-paste without personalization.** Every message must have at least one specific detail about the prospect (their company name, a post they wrote, their community, their title).
2. **A/B test connection requests.** Use Template A for first 20 connections, Template B for the next 20. Track acceptance rates in the social media tracker spreadsheet. Switch to the higher performer.
3. **Wait before DM-ing.** After a connection is accepted, wait 3–5 days before sending a DM. Immediate pitches kill response rates.
4. **Never pitch in the first DM.** The first DM is about establishing a conversation. The pitch comes in the third or fourth interaction.

## Part 2: Initial DM After Connecting (Day 3–5)

**Goal:** Open a conversation, not sell. Position as a resource.
**Do NOT:** Mention pricing, ask for a meeting, or describe services in detail.
**Do:** Reference something specific about them, ask a question, or share something useful.

---

## Part 8: LinkedIn Content Engagement Strategy

Before sending cold DMs to high-value targets, warm them up through their content.

**Steps:**

1. Find target prospect on LinkedIn
2. Follow them (even if not yet connected)
3. Comment thoughtfully on their posts for 2–4 weeks
4. THEN send connection request (mention their post in the request)
5. After connecting, warm DM is much more likely to get a response

**What to comment:**

- Add a specific point they didn't mention ("Great breakdown — one thing I'd add for Phoenix specifically is...")
- Share a related experience without promoting Axle ("We see this with HOA boards all the time in the East Valley...")
- Ask a genuine question ("How have you handled tow disputes when documentation is incomplete?")

**What NOT to comment:**

- Generic praise ("Great post!" or "So true!")
- Anything promotional about Axle Towing
- Anything that makes them feel marketed to
