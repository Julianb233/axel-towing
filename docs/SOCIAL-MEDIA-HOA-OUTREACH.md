## 1. Target Audience Personas

### Persona A: The HOA Board President

**Who they are:**

- Volunteer homeowner, typically 45-65 years old
- Serves as the primary executive decision-maker for all vendor contracts
- Rotates annually or every 2 years — **new opportunities appear constantly**
- Deals with parking complaints at nearly every board meeting
- Makes decisions through community consensus but holds final authority on vendor selection

**Where to find them:**

- LinkedIn (those who list HOA board role publicly)
- NextDoor (they are often tagged or identified as "neighborhood leads")
- Facebook HOA community groups (active admins or frequent posters)
- Arizona Corporation Commission public records (ecorp.azcc.gov)

**Pain points they respond to:**

- "Parking is our #1 resident complaint"
- "We're worried about liability if someone parks in the fire lane"
- "We've been meaning to address this but haven't found a reliable company"
- "Our last towing service was inconsistent — drivers showed up whenever they wanted"

**What motivates them:**

- Zero cost to the HOA budget (critical — they are spending residents' money)
- Looking professional and competent to homeowners
- Stopping the parking complaints so they can focus on real issues
- Legal compliance (they don't want the HOA sued)

**Messaging angle:** "We handle everything — signage, compliance, enforcement — so you can stop getting calls about parking."

---

### Persona B: The Professional Property Manager

**Who they are:**

- Licensed real estate professional (often CAM-certified)
- Manages 5-30+ HOA communities or apartment complexes for a management company
- 30-55 years old, primarily female in HOA management, mixed in multifamily
- Evaluates vendors on reliability, documentation, and communication — not just price
- Has been burned by unreliable towing companies before
- Decides or heavily influences towing vendor selection for their entire portfolio

**Where to find them:**

- LinkedIn (professional identity, searches for industry content)
- Facebook (professional groups like CAI chapters, AMA groups)
- Arizona Multihousing Association (AMA) and Community Associations Institute (CAI) events

**Pain points they respond to:**

- "I need reports I can present to the board without doing extra work"
- "My last tow company ghost-responded at 2 AM and I had to call six times"
- "I manage 12 communities — I need consistency across all of them"
- "Residents are complaining and the board is blaming me"

**What motivates them:**

- Consolidated reporting across all their properties
- One point of contact (not a call center)
- Fast response that protects them from resident escalations
- Making their own job easier and their portfolio look good to property owners

**Messaging angle:** "One call. Monthly reports. Every property. We make you look good to your boards."

---

### Persona C: The HOA Management Company Decision Maker

**Who they are:**

- Operations manager, regional director, or principal of an HOA management company
- Manages vendor relationships and preferred vendor lists at the company level
- A single relationship can open 10-50+ HOA communities simultaneously
- Highly professional; evaluates vendors thoroughly before adding to their preferred list
- Attends CAI Arizona chapter events, Scottsdale AMA events

**Where to find them:**

- LinkedIn (company pages and individual profiles)
- CAI Arizona chapter events and Facebook groups
- HOA management company websites (leadership team pages)

**Pain points they respond to:**

- "We need a towing company we can actually trust across our portfolio"
- "The companies on our current vendor list aren't keeping up with signage compliance"
- "Our property managers are fielding too many parking complaints"

**What motivates them:**

- Being a hero to their own clients (the HOA boards)
- Standardized service across all their properties
- Legal protection and compliance documentation
- A vendor that makes their company look good

**Messaging angle:** "We become your portfolio-wide parking enforcement solution — every community, same standard, one relationship."

## 2. LinkedIn Outreach Strategy

### Platform Overview for This Use Case

LinkedIn is the highest-value platform for HOA property manager outreach. Professional property managers maintain LinkedIn profiles; many HOA board members who are career professionals list their HOA role. The platform supports direct outreach with personalized connection requests and DMs.

### Profile Setup (Before Any Outreach)

Elliott's LinkedIn profile must be optimized before sending a single connection request. Prospects will review the profile before accepting.

**Profile checklist:**

- [ ] Professional headshot (not a selfie — use a photo in front of an Axle truck or in uniform)
- [ ] Headline: "Founder @ Axle Towing & Impound | Private Property Parking Enforcement | Phoenix Metro"
- [ ] About section: 3-4 sentences focused on how Axle helps property managers and HOA boards solve parking problems (not a company bio)
- [ ] Featured section: Link to axletowing.com/services/hoa-towing and a relevant blog post or guide
- [ ] Experience section: Owner/Founder at Axle Towing & Impound since 2021
- [ ] Company page created for "Axle Towing & Impound" — prospects verify through this

### LinkedIn Search Targeting

**Search queries to use in LinkedIn:**

| Target Persona                  | Search Query                                                 |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| HOA Board Members               | "HOA board member" + Phoenix OR Scottsdale OR Arizona        |
| HOA Presidents                  | "homeowners association" + "president" + Arizona             |

## 3. Facebook Groups Strategy

### Why Facebook for This Outreach

Facebook Groups are where HOA board members and property managers actually talk to each other off-the-record. These groups are more candid than LinkedIn. Getting known and trusted in the right groups is extremely high-value because a single recommendation in a group of 500 property managers reaches your exact audience simultaneously.

### Target Groups to Join

**HOA-Specific Groups:**

| Group Type                | Examples to Search                                         |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Arizona HOA groups        | "Arizona HOA homeowners association" "Phoenix HOA board"   |
| Phoenix suburb HOA groups | "Scottsdale HOA" "Gilbert HOA" "Chandler HOA residents"    |
| HOA board member networks | "HOA Board Members Network" "HOA board support"            |
| Arizona community groups  | "Phoenix Homeowners Network" "AZ Neighborhood Association" |

**Property Management Professional Groups:**

| Group Type                  | Examples to Search                                                      |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Arizona property management | "Arizona Property Managers" "Phoenix Property Management Professionals" |
| CAI chapter groups          | "CAI Arizona" "Community Associations Institute AZ"                     |
| AMA-affiliated groups       | "Arizona Multihousing Association members"                              |
| National HOA management     | "HOA Management Professionals" "CAM professionals"                      |

**Local community groups** (where HOA board members participate):

| Group                             | Strategy                                                            |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| r/phoenix Facebook equivalent     | "Phoenix AZ Community" mega-groups                                  |
| City-specific neighborhood groups | Scottsdale, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert community groups                |
| New development community groups  | HOAs for newer subdivisions often have large active Facebook groups |

### Facebook Engagement Protocol

**Phase 1: Observation (Week 1-2 in each group)**

- Join the group but do NOT post or sell immediately
- Read 1-2 weeks of content to understand tone, common questions, and community norms
- React to relevant posts (not spam-liking everything)
- Note what questions/problems come up repeatedly (these are your content topics)

**Phase 2: Value contribution (Week 3-6)**

- Answer questions related to parking, towing, Arizona law — without pitching
- Example: If someone posts "Our HOA has a problem with unauthorized vehicles, any advice?" — answer with specific, actionable information. Mention Axle only if directly asked.
- Share genuinely useful content from axletowing.com blog (frame as a resource, not a promotion)
- Be known as the parking/towing expert, not as a salesperson

**Phase 3: Soft positioning (Week 6+)**

- At this point you are a known member, not a spammer
- You can occasionally post: "We did a free signage audit for an HOA in [nearby area] this week and found 3 compliance issues — happy to share what we found if useful to anyone here"
- Respond to DMs that come inbound from interested group members

## 4. Nextdoor Business Strategy

### Why Nextdoor for HOA Outreach

Nextdoor is uniquely valuable because it is organized by neighborhood — the exact geographic boundaries that HOA communities occupy. HOA board members and residents use Nextdoor constantly to discuss community issues, including parking. It is one of the few platforms where you can reach HOA decision-makers in context (they are literally talking about their neighborhood when you reach them).

**Business profile features:**

- Nextdoor offers a free "Nextdoor Business Page" for local businesses
- Businesses can post and respond in neighborhood feeds with proper verification
- Neighbors can recommend businesses directly to their HOA community
- "Local Deal" posts can promote specific offers (free parking assessment, free signage audit)

### Setup Requirements

1. **Claim a Nextdoor Business Page** at business.nextdoor.com
2. **Service area:** Set to cover all Phoenix metro zip codes Axle serves
3. **Business category:** Towing Service / Automotive Services
4. **Profile:** Professional description focused on HOA and property management enforcement
5. **Logo and cover photo:** Use professional branded images
6. **Link to axletowing.com**

### Nextdoor Engagement Strategy

**How Nextdoor works for B2B outreach:**

Nextdoor allows business accounts to post in neighborhoods where they are verified. The key is appearing helpful in conversations, not advertising.

**Step 1: Monitor neighborhood feeds**

- Watch for posts mentioning: parking, towing, unauthorized vehicles, fire lanes, HOA violations, parking complaints
- These are warm leads — someone is actively experiencing the problem Axle solves

**Step 2: Respond helpfully**
When a resident or board member posts about a parking problem:

```
Hi [Name],

This is Elliott with Axle Towing & Impound — we work with several HOA
communities in [neighborhood/area] on parking enforcement.

For unauthorized vehicles in a gated HOA, here's what typically works:
[Give specific, useful advice — don't just say "call us"]

The key thing your board needs is compliant towing authorization
signage under ARS 9-499.05. If that's not in place yet, any tow can
be challenged. Happy to answer questions — feel free to DM me or
call 480-288-5526.
```

**Step 3: Offer free assessments via "Local Deals"**

Nextdoor's "Local Deal" feature allows verified businesses to post neighborhood-specific offers. Post a free offer once per quarter:

```
FREE: HOA Parking Enforcement Assessment

We're offering complimentary parking enforcement assessments for HOA
communities in [specific neighborhoods].

What you get:
✓ Signage compliance check (are your signs legally compliant?)
✓ Parking problem assessment (which areas are highest violation zones)
✓ Enforcement plan recommendation (no obligation to hire us)

We serve HOA communities across Phoenix Metro at zero cost to the HOA.
Towing fees from violators cover our service.

Call 480-288-5526 or message us here to schedule.
— Elliott, Axle Towing & Impound
```

**Step 4: Collect recommendations**
Nextdoor has a business "recommendation" feature. After completing work for a community:

- Ask satisfied HOA board members or property managers to leave a recommendation on Nextdoor
- These recommendations are visible to everyone in the neighborhood — powerful social proof for nearby HOAs

## 5. Instagram Content Strategy

### Role of Instagram in the HOA B2B Funnel

Instagram is a brand awareness and trust-building platform for this use case — not a direct B2B lead generation tool. HOA board members and property managers do use Instagram personally, and they WILL check Axle's Instagram if they've heard about the company. An active, professional Instagram page signals legitimacy and professionalism to prospects who find it during their vetting process.

Additionally, Instagram is effective for:

- Reaching younger property managers (25-40 demographic)
- Showcasing the trucks, team, and operations professionally
- Building local brand awareness in Phoenix neighborhoods
- Retargeting website visitors (if running paid ads later)

### Account Setup

- **Handle:** @axletowing or @axletowingphoenix (whichever is available)
- **Bio format:**
  ```
  Axle Towing & Impound 🔧
  Private Property Parking Enforcement
  HOA • Apartments • Commercial Properties
  Phoenix Metro AZ | 480-288-5526
  🔗 axletowing.com
  ```
- **Profile photo:** Logo or a professional photo of Elliott with a truck
- **Highlights:** Create Story Highlights for: HOA Services, Reviews, How It Works, Arizona Law

### Content Pillars and Post Types

**Pillar 1: Education (30% of posts)**
Content that HOA board members or property managers would find genuinely useful. Designed to be saved and shared.

Examples:

- "5 signs your HOA's parking enforcement isn't legally compliant" (carousel)
- "What happens if your towing signs aren't up to code in Arizona" (infographic)
- "The difference between a parking warning and a tow in Arizona" (short video)
- "How HOA parking enforcement actually works — step by step" (carousel)

**Pillar 2: Social proof (25% of posts)**
Real results, real testimonials, real communities (with permission).

Examples:

- Screenshot of a property manager Google review with a short caption
- "60 days after enforcement started: violations down 85%" stat graphic
- "This HOA board stopped getting parking complaints at their meetings" story post
- Before/after: "Parking lot with chronic violations → same lot 6 weeks after enforcement"

**Pillar 3: Behind the scenes (25% of posts)**
Humanizes the brand. Elliott and the team, real trucks, real work.

Examples:

- Photo: Elliot doing a property walkthrough for a free assessment
- Reel: A day in the life of an Axle Towing enforcement patrol
- Photo: The truck fleet
- Photo: Team at the Arizona Multihousing Association event
- Short video: How we document a violation before a tow

**Pillar 4: Local and community (20% of posts)**
Phoenix-area content that signals you belong here.

Examples:

- "We just added [neighborhood] to our service area" post
- Photo from a community event or HOA meeting (if invited as a vendor)
- "Phoenix's multifamily housing boom means more parking enforcement needs than ever" stat post
- Arizona-specific seasonal content (monsoon season, spring HOA meetings, etc.)

### Instagram Posting Cadence

- **Feed posts:** 3-4 per week
- **Stories:** Daily (behind the scenes, quick tips, reposts of tags)
- **Reels:** 1-2 per week (higher reach than feed posts)

## 8. Platform Priority Stack

Given Elliott's time constraints as a business owner, execute platforms in priority order. Do not try to do everything at once.

**Priority 1 — LinkedIn (Weeks 1-4 before other channels)**
Highest conversion potential for the B2B HOA/property manager audience. Every hour on LinkedIn yields better B2B results than any other platform for this use case.

**Priority 2 — Facebook Groups (Start Week 2)**
Highest reach within the target audience at zero cost. Groups where property managers talk to each other are extremely high-value. Requires patience (no selling for first 2 weeks in each group).

**Priority 3 — Nextdoor (Start Week 2 in parallel with Facebook)**
Uniquely positioned for hyper-local HOA outreach. Neighbors recommending Axle to HOA board members in the same neighborhood is the best word-of-mouth amplification available.

**Priority 4 — Instagram (Start Week 3)**
Brand awareness and legitimacy signaling. Important but lower direct ROI for B2B HOA outreach. Build it consistently but don't over-invest time here at the expense of LinkedIn.

**Time budget recommendation:**

- LinkedIn: 45 min/day (30 min content + DMs, 15 min connection requests)
- Facebook Groups: 20 min/day
- Nextdoor: 15 min every other day
- Instagram: 30 min every other day (batch content creation once a week)
