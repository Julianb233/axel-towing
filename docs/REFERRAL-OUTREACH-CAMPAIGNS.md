## Objection Handling Scripts

### For HOA Property Managers

**"We already have a towing company."**

> "That's great — can I ask how long you've been with them? The reason I ask is that a lot of the management companies we end up working with had someone, but responsiveness was inconsistent or the monthly reporting wasn't there. We're not asking you to switch today — just asking for a chance to show you what we do differently. Would a one-property trial make sense?"

**"I don't make that decision — the board does."**

> "Totally understand. If you're open to it, I'd love to get you a one-pager you could share with the board. That way when the topic comes up at the next meeting, you've got something concrete to present. Can I send that to your email?"

**"We've had bad experiences with towing companies before."**

> "I hear that a lot, honestly. The impound industry has a reputation problem. The bad actors are why we lead with Arizona compliance documentation, monthly reports, and a dedicated contact — not a dispatch center. Would it be worth a 15-minute call so you can see if we're different?"

**"We're not looking right now."**

> "No problem at all. When would be a better time to revisit this? I'll set a reminder to follow up then — don't want to bother you before you're ready."

---

### For Auto Mechanics

**"I don't really refer my customers anywhere."**

> "Makes total sense — and this isn't a hard referral arrangement. It's more like: if a customer needs a tow and asks if you know anyone, you have a name to give them. And when we're towing a broken-down vehicle, we give your name. Zero obligation. Does that feel more comfortable?"

**"I have a towing company I already use."**

> "That's fine — this isn't about changing who you use for your shop's vehicles. It's about who you recommend to customers who ask. If one of your customers is stranded and needs a tow, would you rather have a local number to give them or tell them to Google it?"

**"I'm not interested in referral programs."**

> "No pressure at all. If you ever need a reliable tow for a customer's vehicle — 24/7 — here's my direct line: [Phone]. We're there when you need us."

---

### For Locksmiths

**"I don't get calls that need towing."**

> "More than you might think — dead batteries are a common combo with lockouts, and some customers just ask who to call for a tow while you're still on-site. If you had a card to hand them, it closes the loop for them. Would it be worth having a handful of our cards to keep in your van?"

**"I'm too busy to manage a referral program."**

> "There's nothing to manage. If you get a call that needs a tow, you text or call us. That's it. No tracking, no forms. We pick up 24/7."

---

### For Paving Companies

**"We handle vehicle notices ourselves / property manager handles it."**

> "Sometimes it works great. But when it doesn't — when 8 cars are still in the lot when your crew shows up — that's where we come in. We're available same-day for jobs where notices didn't work. Would it make sense to have our number as a backup?"

**"It's too expensive."**

> "Fair point. Let me ask: how much does a 2-hour crew delay cost you? For most jobs we can clear a lot for less than what an hour of delay costs in labor and equipment. Happy to talk through the numbers on a specific upcoming job."
