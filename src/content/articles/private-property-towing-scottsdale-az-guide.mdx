## How Private Property Towing Works in Scottsdale

Private property towing in Arizona is governed by ARS 9-499.05, which grants property owners the legal right to remove unauthorized vehicles from their land. The law applies equally across all cities in the state, including Scottsdale, and the process is straightforward when done correctly.

Here is how it works, step by step:

### Step 1: Post Compliant Signage

Arizona law requires that private property towing signage meet specific standards before any vehicle can be legally removed. Under ARS 9-499.05, your signs must:

- Be posted at each vehicle entrance to the property
- Display the name and phone number of the towing company
- Include the address of the storage facility where vehicles will be taken
- Be clearly visible and legible from at least 50 feet away
- Meet minimum size requirements (the statute does not specify exact dimensions, but industry standard is 18" x 24" or larger)

For Scottsdale HOA communities with gated entries, signs should be posted at the main vehicle gate and any secondary access points. For shopping centers and commercial properties along Scottsdale Road or the Airpark, signs must be visible at every parking lot entrance.

### Step 2: Identify the Unauthorized Vehicle

The property owner, manager, or authorized agent (such as an HOA board member or security guard) must identify a vehicle that is in violation of the posted parking rules. Common violations in Scottsdale include:

- Non-resident vehicles parked in assigned resident spaces
- Vehicles without valid HOA parking permits
- Cars parked in fire lanes, handicap spaces (without proper placard), or designated no-parking zones
- Abandoned vehicles that have not moved in 96+ hours
- Commercial vehicles parked in residential areas in violation of CC&Rs
- Oversized vehicles (RVs, boats, trailers) in restricted areas

## What Private Property Towing Costs Scottsdale Property Owners

The answer surprises most property managers who call us for the first time: **private property towing costs Scottsdale property owners absolutely nothing.**

Under the private property impound model used throughout Arizona, all towing and storage costs are recovered from the vehicle owner when they retrieve their car. The property owner, HOA, or management company pays zero out-of-pocket fees. This is not a promotional offer — it is how the entire PPI industry operates under Arizona law.

According to ARS 28-3511, towing companies may charge the vehicle owner reasonable towing and storage fees. In the Phoenix metro area, typical fees include:

## Arizona Legal Framework for Private Property Towing

All private property towing in Scottsdale is governed by state law. The two key statutes every property owner should understand:

### ARS 9-499.05 — Removal of Vehicles from Private Property

This is the primary statute authorizing private property towing in Arizona. Key provisions include:

- Property owners may authorize towing of unauthorized vehicles from private property
- Compliant signage must be posted at vehicle entrances before towing can occur
- The towing company must be licensed and authorized by the property owner
- Towed vehicles must be stored at a licensed facility within 15 miles of the city limits (or the nearest available facility)
- The vehicle owner has the right to retrieve personal belongings from the vehicle during business hours

### ARS 28-3511 — Towing Fees and Charges

This statute governs what towing companies may charge vehicle owners for private property impounds. It establishes that fees must be "reasonable" and sets the framework for daily storage maximums.

> **Key legal fact:** Under Arizona law, a property owner who authorizes a tow in good faith and in compliance with ARS 9-499.05 signage requirements is protected from civil liability related to the tow. This means that as long as your signage is compliant and the tow is authorized by someone with legal authority over the property, you are legally protected.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can my Scottsdale HOA tow vehicles without warning?

Yes, if compliant signage is posted under ARS 9-499.05. The posted signs themselves serve as the legal warning. However, many Scottsdale HOAs choose to place courtesy warning notices on vehicles before calling for a tow, as a matter of community relations. This is a best practice but not a legal requirement. Your CC&Rs may also specify a warning period — check with your HOA attorney.

### Does Scottsdale have any city-specific towing ordinances beyond state law?

Scottsdale does not impose significant additional regulations on private property towing beyond what ARS 9-499.05 requires. The city's municipal code primarily addresses parking on public streets and city-owned lots. Private property towing is governed by state law. However, Scottsdale's zoning code may affect signage placement and size for certain property types, particularly in the Scottsdale Civic Center and Old Town overlay districts.

### What documents do I need to set up private property towing for my Scottsdale property?

To establish a towing agreement with Axle Towing for your Scottsdale property, you will need: (1) proof of property ownership or management authority (deed, management agreement, or HOA board resolution), (2) a signed towing authorization agreement, and (3) confirmation that compliant signage is posted at all vehicle entrances. We provide free signage consultation to ensure your signs meet ARS 9-499.05 requirements before any towing begins.

### Can short-term rental (Airbnb) guests' vehicles be towed from HOA parking?

If the guest's vehicle is parked in violation of HOA rules and the CC&Rs restrict or regulate short-term rentals, yes. The vehicle can be towed under the same authority as any other unauthorized vehicle, provided compliant signage is posted. This has become a significant issue for many Scottsdale HOAs, particularly in communities near Old Town, the Scottsdale Quarter, and Kierland. Work with your HOA attorney to ensure your parking rules explicitly address STR guest vehicles.
