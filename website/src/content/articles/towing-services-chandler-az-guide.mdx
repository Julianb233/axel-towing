## Towing Services Available in Chandler

### Private Property Towing (Impound Services)

Private property towing is the most common type of towing service in Chandler. Property owners and managers use this service to remove unauthorized vehicles from parking lots, garages, and private roadways.

**How it works in Chandler:**

1. The property owner or manager contracts with a licensed towing company
2. Compliant signage is posted at all entrances per Arizona law (ARS 9-499.05)
3. When unauthorized vehicles are identified, the towing company is dispatched
4. The vehicle is towed to a storage yard and the vehicle owner is responsible for retrieval fees
5. The property owner pays nothing -- the service is free for property owners

**Key Chandler regulations:**
Chandler follows Arizona state law (ARS 9-499.05) for private property towing, with local ordinances that may impose additional requirements on signage placement, fee limits, and notification procedures. Property managers in Chandler should verify compliance with both state and local requirements.

Vehicle relocation is a distinct service from impound towing. In Chandler, property managers use relocation services for:

- You are entitled to an itemized receipt
- If proper signage was not posted, the tow may have been illegal

## Chandler Parking Regulations and Towing Laws

### Arizona State Law (Applies Throughout Chandler)

- **ARS 9-499.05:** Requires proper signage, 24-hour vehicle release, acceptance of multiple payment methods
- **ARS 28-3511:** Governs law enforcement-ordered impounds
- **HB 2269:** Updated signage size and content requirements

### Chandler City Code

Chandler's municipal code includes additional provisions for:

- **Parking on public streets:** Chandler prohibits parking on most residential streets between 2:00 AM and 6:00 AM without a permit in certain areas
- **Oversized vehicle parking:** Restrictions on parking RVs, boats, and trailers on residential streets
- **Commercial vehicle parking:** Limits on parking commercial vehicles in residential neighborhoods
- **Handicap parking enforcement:** Chandler enforces ADA parking compliance on both public and private property
- **Abandoned vehicle reporting:** Chandler residents can report abandoned vehicles through the city's code enforcement division

### Chandler Police Towing

Chandler Police may order tows for:
- Vehicles involved in traffic stops (DUI, suspended license, no insurance)
- Vehicles blocking traffic or creating a hazard
- Vehicles parked illegally on public streets
- Abandoned vehicles on public property

Police-ordered tows in Chandler follow ARS 28-3511 procedures and may involve mandatory hold periods.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How quickly can a towing company respond to a call in Chandler?

Most private property towing companies, including Axle Towing, can respond to Chandler locations within 25-35 minutes depending on traffic conditions and time of day. During peak rush hours (particularly on the Loop 101/202 corridor), response times may be slightly longer. After-hours calls (late night/early morning) typically have faster response times due to lighter traffic.

### Is private property towing free for Chandler property owners?

Yes. Private property impound towing is free for property owners throughout Chandler and the entire Phoenix metro area. The towing company recovers its costs from impound fees charged to the vehicle owner when they retrieve their vehicle. The property owner, HOA, or management company pays nothing for the tow, the signage, or the ongoing enforcement service.

### What signs are required for legal towing in Chandler?

Under Arizona law (ARS 9-499.05 and HB 2269), private property towing signs must be posted at every vehicle entrance to the property. Signs must include the words "Tow-Away Zone" or equivalent, the towing company's name and phone number, and a statement that unauthorized vehicles will be towed at the vehicle owner's expense. Signs must meet minimum size requirements and be clearly visible at all times, including at night.

### How much does it cost to get a towed car back in Chandler?

For a standard private property impound in the Chandler area, vehicle owners typically pay $200-$350 if they retrieve the vehicle the same day. This includes the tow fee ($175-$275), possible administrative fees ($25-$50), and one day of storage ($15-$30). Storage fees accumulate daily, so retrieving your vehicle as quickly as possible saves money.

### Can my HOA tow cars from the street in Chandler?

HOA towing authority in Chandler depends on whether the streets are private (owned by the HOA) or public (maintained by the City of Chandler). HOAs can generally enforce towing on private streets and in common area parking lots. Towing from public streets typically requires involvement from Chandler Police or city code enforcement. Review your HOA's CC&Rs and consult with the HOA's legal counsel for guidance specific to your community.
