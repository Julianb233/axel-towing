# The Complete Map of Phoenix Impound Lots & Tow Yards (2026)

When your car gets towed in the Phoenix metro area, the first question is always the same: "Where is my car?" The answer depends on who towed it and where it was towed from. Phoenix and its surrounding cities have dozens of impound lots and tow yards, each serving different purposes — private property impounds, police-ordered tows, accident recovery, and abandoned vehicle storage.

This guide is the most comprehensive directory of impound lots and tow yards in the Phoenix metropolitan area. We have compiled the name, address, phone number, and primary function of every major facility so you can locate your vehicle quickly.

## How to Find Your Towed Vehicle

Before scrolling through the full directory, use these steps to narrow down where your car is:

### Step 1: Determine Who Towed You

| Situation | Who Towed You | Where to Call First |
|-----------|--------------|:-------------------:|
| Parked at apartment complex, shopping center, or private lot | Private property impound (PPI) company | Check the signage at the property |
| Parked on a public street illegally | City police department | Call the city PD non-emergency line |
| Involved in a car accident | Police-contracted tow company | Call the responding police department |
| Vehicle abandoned or reported stolen | Police impound | Call the reporting agency |
| Parked in a tow-away zone on a highway | Arizona DPS or highway patrol | (602) 223-2000 |

### Step 2: Check the Signage or Police Report

- **Private property tow:** Go back to where you parked and read the towing company sign. It must list the company name, phone number, and storage facility address.
- **Police tow:** The police report or citation will list the towing company and storage location.
- **Highway tow:** Arizona DPS contracts with specific towing companies per district.

### Step 3: Call the Towing Company

Once you know which company towed your vehicle, call them directly. Most companies have online vehicle lookup tools.

> **Key cost fact:** According to the Arizona Department of Transportation, the average cost to retrieve a towed vehicle in Maricopa County is $347 when picked up within 48 hours. That number rises to $500+ after 5 days due to accumulating storage fees. Retrieve your vehicle as soon as possible.

### Your Rights at the Impound Lot

Under Arizona law (ARS 28-3511):
- You can retrieve personal belongings during business hours, even if you cannot pay the full fees
- You are entitled to an itemized fee breakdown
- The facility must accept multiple payment methods
- Fees must be "reasonable" and consistent with industry standards
- If you believe the tow was illegal, you can dispute it through the Arizona Department of Weights and Measures at (602) 542-4977

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I find out which impound lot has my car?

Start with where your car was parked. If it was on private property, check the towing signage at the location — it must list the towing company and storage facility. If it was towed by police, call the non-emergency number for the police department that has jurisdiction where your car was parked (Phoenix PD: 602-262-6151, Tempe PD: 480-350-8311, Mesa PD: 480-644-2211, etc.). If you parked at a location served by Axle Towing, use our vehicle lookup at [axletowing.com/locate-vehicle](/locate-vehicle) or call (480) 288-5526.

### Can I retrieve my car on weekends?

It depends on the facility. Axle Towing offers after-hours and weekend vehicle releases by appointment (a gate fee applies). Many other impound lots have limited weekend hours or no weekend access. Always call ahead to confirm hours before making the trip.

### What happens if I cannot afford to get my car out of impound?

Storage fees accumulate daily, which makes waiting more expensive. If you cannot pay immediately: (1) Contact the towing company to ask about payment arrangements — many will work with you, (2) retrieve your personal belongings (this is your legal right), (3) check if you have roadside assistance or towing coverage through your car insurance or credit card, and (4) be aware that after the statutory holding period (typically 10-30 days depending on the situation), the vehicle may be processed for lien sale or auction.

### How long before my towed car is auctioned?

Under Arizona law, a towing company must hold a privately towed vehicle for a minimum of 10 days after proper notification to the registered owner before initiating lien proceedings. For police-ordered impounds, the hold period may be 30 days or longer (especially for DUI or evidence holds). After the hold period and proper legal process, the vehicle may be sold at auction. The registered owner receives written notice before any sale.

### Is there a way to get towing fees waived?

Towing fees are generally not waived unless the tow was improper (no signage, unauthorized tow, wrong vehicle). If you believe the tow was illegal, gather evidence (photos of the location, signage, your parking spot) and file a dispute. If the tow was legal but you are experiencing financial hardship, some towing companies offer payment plans — ask directly.
