## How the Program Works — Zero Cost to Your HOA

Axle Towing provides a complete parking-enforcement program to qualifying HOA
communities at no cost to the association. The program is funded entirely by
impound fees collected from the owners of vehicles that violated posted
restrictions — not by dues, assessments, or the board.

What's included at no charge:

- ARS-compliant towing signage, designed and installed
- Regular patrols tailored to your community's schedule
- Unauthorized-vehicle removal, documented end-to-end
- Online portal access for the board and the management company
- Monthly activity reports for board meetings
- Resident communication templates (notice letters, FAQ handouts)
- 24/7 dispatch for urgent situations
- Legal documentation for every tow

The only people who pay Axle are drivers who park where they are not allowed to
park. Homeowners in good standing never pay a cent.

## Fire-Lane & Life-Safety Enforcement

Fire-lane blocking is one of the most common — and most dangerous — parking
violations in HOA communities. It creates insurance exposure for the board and
genuine safety risk for residents.

Axle's program includes:

- Fire-lane signage that meets Arizona Fire Code marking requirements
- Priority dispatch for fire-lane blockages (response targeted within 30 minutes
  during business hours)
- Coordination with local fire districts when they flag a lane during an
  inspection
- Documented photo evidence of every fire-lane violation for your board's
  insurance carrier

This alone is typically enough to justify adopting a formal program at the next
board meeting.

## No Long-Term Contract

Axle does not ask HOAs to sign multi-year agreements, and there is no
cancellation fee. Programs run month-to-month with a short written authorization.
If the board ever decides the program isn't serving the community, you give us
30 days' notice and we close it cleanly — remove signage, hand over records,
done.

We believe the only reason a community should keep this program in place is
because it is working. If it is not working, we would rather you tell us so we
can fix it, or part ways respectfully.

## Board Adoption Process

Most HOAs move from first phone call to first patrol within 3-4 weeks:

1. **Week 1 — Board inquiry.** A board member calls us or submits the inquiry
   form on axletowing.com/partners/hoa-boards. We send a program overview and
   schedule a 30-minute call with the board president or management company.
2. **Week 1-2 — Community assessment.** We walk (or drive) the community,
   review your CC&Rs and parking rules, and draft a custom enforcement plan.
   You receive a board presentation package.
3. **Week 2 — Board vote.** Present the program at a regular board meeting.
   Because there is no cost, no long-term contract, and Axle handles the legal
   compliance, most boards approve on a single meeting.
4. **Week 3 — Signage & resident notice.** We install ARS-compliant signage
   and distribute resident communication (notice letter + FAQ). Residents
   typically get 15-30 days of notice before enforcement begins.
5. **Week 3-4 — Enforcement begins.** Regular patrols start. The board gets
   portal access and the first monthly report the following month.

## What We Need From the Board

To quote a program for your community we'll need:

- A copy of the parking sections of your CC&Rs or Rules & Regulations
- A map of common areas, guest parking, and any reserved spaces
- Any known problem areas (fire lanes, guest-spot abuse, commercial vehicles,
  inoperable vehicles, etc.)
- A board or management-company point of contact

Most boards already have this on file. If yours does not, we can help assemble
it during the community assessment.
